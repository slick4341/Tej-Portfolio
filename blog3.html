Essential Laws of UX for Web and Mobile Design

Posted 17 July 2025

While good design often feels intuitive, it is grounded in principles that guide how users perceive and interact with interfaces. The Laws of UX are psychological principles that help designers create more effective, user-friendly experiences.

Understanding these laws not only improves usability but also ensures that your designs align with how people naturally think and behave. Here are some key UX laws to consider when designing for web and mobile.

“Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.” — Steve Jobs

Below are some fundamental Laws of UX that can enhance your web and mobile designs:

  • Hick’s Law — The more choices a user has, the longer it takes to make a decision. Keep it simple.
  • Fitts’s Law — The time to reach a target depends on its size and distance. Make key actions easy to tap or click.
  • Law of Proximity — Elements that are close together are perceived as related. Use spacing intentionally.
  • Miller’s Law — People can only hold about 7±2 items in memory. Avoid overwhelming your users with too much information.
  • Jakob’s Law — Users expect your site or app to work like others they already know. Follow established patterns where appropriate.

Applying these laws helps create designs that feel natural and effortless to use. Whether on a website or a mobile app, keeping these principles in mind can elevate your work and delight your users.
